About Levi’s (History)

Levi Strauss & Co. was founded in 1853 in San Francisco by Levi Strauss.

In 1873, tailor Jacob Davis partnered with Strauss to patent copper-riveted work pants—creating the first modern blue jeans. This iconic style evolved into the Levi’s® 501®, one of the most recognizable garments in history.

From durable workwear to global fashion staple, Levi’s has influenced music, film, and youth culture for generations, while pursuing innovations in fit, fabric, and more responsible finishing techniques.
